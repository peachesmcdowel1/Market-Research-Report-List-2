<p><p>Microscope digital cameras are widely available in two main types: CMOS and CCD. CMOS cameras are known for their low power consumption, high integration, and cost-effectiveness. They provide good image quality and are ideal for applications that require high speed and high resolution. On the other hand, CCD cameras are known for their high sensitivity, low noise, and good image quality. They are suitable for applications that require high-quality images, such as fluorescence imaging and live-cell microscopy. Each type has its own strengths and capabilities, making them suitable for different applications in the market.</p></p>
