<p><ul><li>Assets Backed Securities (ABS)</li><li>Collateralized Debt Obligations (CDO)</li><li>Mortgage-Backed Securities (MBS)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Structured finance markets involve the trading of complex financial instruments backed by various types of assets. Assets Backed Securities (ABS) are created by pooling together assets such as loans or receivables and selling them to investors. Collateralized Debt Obligations (CDO) bundle various types of debt and divide them into different tranches with varying levels of risk and return. Mortgage-Backed Securities (MBS) are securities backed by a pool of mortgages, providing investors with regular payments based on the underlying mortgage payments.</p></p>
